  • Bahía de Banderas

    Bahía de Banderas is a natural bay of Mexico located on the coast of the Pacific Ocean, between the states of Jalisco and Nayarit, and gives its name to one of the municipalities of Nayarit, Bahía de Banderas.

    The bay has a territorial extension of 773,497 km², is delimited, to the north, by Punta Mita, and to the south by Cape Corrientes and is one of the deepest bays in the world (more than 900 m).

    The bay's climate is warm subhumid, with summer rains from June to September and the average annual rainfall is 1,159.2 mm. The average annual temperature ranges between 23.1 ºC and 27.8 ºC. 90% of the days of the year are sunny.

    The vegetation composed of palm trees, amates and mangroves; in the medium and high jungle of huanacaxtle, capomales, spring, cedar and amapa trees; and in the upper parts of oak, white wood, oak and pine.

    Among the most interesting and unique animals in the bay area are: the nine-banded armadillo (Dasypus novemcinctus), the white-nosed coati (Nasua narica); also four important felines: jaguar (Panthera onca), ocelot (Leopardus pardalis), tigrillo (Leopardus tigrinus) and jaguarundi (Puma yagouaroundi); and a great variety of reptiles: green iguana (Iguana iguana), black iguana (Ctenosaura pectinata) and the enchaquirad0 lizard (Heloderma horridum), river crocodile (Crocodylus acutus) and a variety of different species of snakes.

    Among the birds we can find wonderful species such as Cacique Mexicano (Cacicus melanicterus), Luis bienteveo (Pitangus sulphuratus), Bonnet magpie (Calocitta colliei), Yellow Chest (Pitangus sulphuratus), Blue-footed booby bird (Sula nebouxii), Brown pelican ( Pelecanus occidentalis, yellow-legged heron (Egretta thula), earwig frigatebird (Fregata magnificens), night heron (Nyctanassa violacea), neotropical cormorant (Phalacrocorax brasilianus), American white ibis (Eudocimus albus), American black vulture (Coragyps atratus), among many other species. Marine life abounds in this area and we can find species such as blue marlin (Makaira mazara), black marlin (Makaira indica), snapper (Lutjanus campechanus), sailfish (Istiophorus platypterus), tuna and croaker, bottlenose dolphins or nosed dolphins. bottlenose dolphins (Tursiops truncatus), spotted dolphins (Stenella attenuata) and spinner dolphins (Stenella longirostris).

    And of course, the incredible humpback whales (Megaptera novaeangliae), which return to the area every winter, to enjoy the warm waters from December to April. The whales migrate to three main areas, the Bay of Banderas, the Revillagigedo Islands and Hawaii.

    The 5 most common species of turtle in the bay are the olive ridley turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea), the delicate leatherback turtle (Dermochelys coriacea), the green turtle (Chelonia agassizii) and the hawksbill turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata).
